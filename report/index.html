1. One-Paragraph Summary

This Kaggle competition asks us to predict TVT (true vertical thickness — the geological-depth coordinate) at every measured-depth (MD) point of a horizontal wellbore beyond a known Prediction Start (PS) point. Inputs available at scoring time: the well's 3D trajectory (X, Y, Z, MD), a noisy gamma-ray log (GR, ~28% missing on average), the known TVT before PS, and a paired vertical reference log (typewell) of GR vs TVT. The task is most naturally framed as a geophysical inverse problem: given the observed GR along the lateral and a typewell GR–depth dictionary, recover the wellbore's geological position. Naive baselines reach median per-well RMSE ≈ 10.7 ft; competitive approaches combine signal alignment (DTW), Tikhonov-regularized GR inversion, sequential filtering, and ML sequence models with auxiliary formation-top supervision. State-of-the-art literature (Ambrus et al. 2022, NORCE) uses a Mixture Density Network for direct multi-modal probabilistic inversion at 443 ms / step.

3. Dataset Snapshot

The dataset is uniform-1-ft-MD-sampled with identical schema across all 773 training wells. Median well asks us to extend ~74% of its length forward from PS. Geological dips are mild on average but the tail is steep — any predictor that assumes monotonic or zero dip will fail on a sizable fraction of wells.

4. Six Domain Insights

Insight 1

This is an inverse problem, not a regression task

Reframe as: recover wellbore position h(MD) = TVT by inverting the forward operator F(h) := GR_v(h(MD)) against observed horizontal GR. Tikhonov / Gauss–Newton template solves this in < 200 lines of NumPy (see Reference §7).

Insight 2

Shift & stretch = the geosteering question

A horizontal-well GR snippet matches the typewell GR at the TVT range it was drilled in, modulo a small affine warp for local dip. This is the geosteering engineer's mental algorithm and algorithmically is subsequence DTW / cross-correlation.

Insight 3

Nearby wells' typewells are offset copies of the same log

773 typewells share only 28 distinct geology labels, dominated by the same 6 formations. Neighbouring typewells are the same vertical GR pattern shifted in depth. Stack the K nearest typewells to get a cleaner reference signal.

Insight 4

Sequential inversion: march one MD point at a time

The standard LWD workflow reuses the previous MD point's recovered TVT as the initial guess for the next. Maps to Kalman / particle filter / sequential MDN inference.

Insight 5

Formation-top columns are training-only ground truth

Columns ANCC, ASTNU, ASTNL, EGFDU, EGFDL, BUDA hold the exact elevation of each of the six formation tops at each MD point. Training-only. Use as auxiliary multi-task supervision in any ML model.

Insight 6

Test set is hidden — code generalizes, not memorizes

The 3 wells under test/ are a submission-shape template. The real evaluation runs on a hidden set on Kaggle's servers. Implication: never hard-code well IDs; load test/ by directory scan; trust leave-one-well-out CV over the public LB.

5. Formal Inverse-Problem Formulation

Tikhonov-regularized minimization (Schumann, 2021 — see §7):

minh Φ(h) = ‖Wd(F(h) − dobs)‖₂² + β‖Wm h‖₂²

The Gauss–Newton normal equation for the per-iteration model update Δh is

(JT WdT Wd J + β WmT Wm) Δh = −∇Φ

where J = ∂F/∂h is the Jacobian, Wd down-weights noisy / missing GR rows, and Wm is a finite-difference operator that penalizes TVT-path curvature. β is picked via L-curve criterion or cross-validation. The full derivation with figures lives in competition_overview.html §5.

6. Method Portfolio

MethodVerdictUse it for
Constant Floor — median RMSE ≈ 10.7 ft First sanity-check submission.
Linear-Z Cuts RMSE roughly in half — median ≈ 35.4 ft Quick second-pass; reuses lateral's own geometry.
DTW subseq match Strong classical baseline; interpretable; no training. First “real” submission. Especially with stacked typewells.
Tikhonov Gauss–Newton Mathematically clean; handles missing GR. Needs a good initialization — use DTW seed or previous-point solution.
Particle / Kalman Robust to multimodal posteriors; online. When linearized inversion exposes local-minimum problems.
MDN sequential State-of-the-art (Ambrus et al. 2022); 443 ms / step. If you have GPU + time: tracks N candidate SVD curves with explicit probabilities. See Reference §7.
Sequence model Powerful but 773 wells is small without strong priors. Train with auxiliary heads on the 6 formation-top columns.
Hybrid (inv. → ML residual) Combines physical fidelity with pattern flexibility. Best ceiling; two-stage but stable.

7. Reference Implementations & Literature

classical inversion

LWD_inversion — Tikhonov GR inversion

github.com/hhschumann/LWD_inversion (Schumann, 2021). ~200 LOC Python/Jupyter. Implements eq. (1)–(6) (in competition_overview.html) with L-curve regularization. Forward model: nearest-neighbor lookup on a pilot/typewell GR. Known weakness: local-minimum trap from linearization → mitigate with sequential initialization.

subsequence alignment

DTW on GR logs — dtw-python recipe

The classical signal-alignment baseline. From notebook/DTW.ipynb (gitignored third-party reference):

!pip install dtw-python
from dtw import dtw, rabinerJuangStepPattern

w1 = horizontal_well_gr   # numpy array (post-PS GR)
w2 = typewell_gr          # numpy array (vertical reference GR)
alignment = dtw(
    w1, w2,
    keep_internals=True,
    step_pattern=rabinerJuangStepPattern(1, "c"),
)
alignment.plot(type="twoway", offset=-200)

Directly applicable: feed in HW.GR (post-PS) against the typewell (or a super-typewell — see Insight 3); read off TVT by indexing the typewell by the alignment indices.

state-of-the-art

Ambrus et al. 2022 — sequential MDN multi-modal inversion

AI-based multi-modal interpretation of logs for ahead-of-bit probabilistic ROP prediction. Presented at NFES / NORCE Geosteering & Formation Evaluation Workshop 2022 · PDF (local copy).

  • Model: Mixture Density Network outputting N SVD (= TVT) candidates + probability per candidate.
  • Training: 28 M synthetic SVD samples with variable dips and faulting; loss = α · I_class + I_MAE (cross-entropy on closest mode + L1 on closest mode).
  • Sequential interpretation: previous step's candidates seed the next step; coinciding points merge with summed probability; low-probability realizations are dropped.
  • Speed: 443 ms / step on standard CPU; ~100× faster than classical stratigraphy interpretation.
  • Validation: Geosteering World Cup 2020 semi-final well (Middle Woodford fm, South Central Oklahoma).

Sketch: at the bit position, N candidate SVD curves branch out ahead, each with its own probability. The classical DTW gives one path; the MDN gives a distribution.

production reference

ROGII StarSteer — Auto Steer

rogii.com · commercial geosteering software. Its “Auto Steer” module outputs exactly the TVD-vs-MD cross-section + per-MD confidence the competition is asking for. The competition is effectively a public benchmark of that auto-interpretation task.

DTW intuition — visual

DTW (dynamic time warping) finds a monotone many-to-one tie between two sequences that minimizes the cumulative point-to-point distance. For GR logs, that means matching geological signatures even when one sequence is locally stretched or compressed relative to the other (= local dip).

9. Pipeline Constraints & Pitfalls

  • Hidden test: code must be well-id agnostic — scan the directory.
  • CV: Leave-One-Well-Out or GroupKFold by XY cluster. Random row splits leak.
  • GR missingness is blocky in many wells — use a mask, not mean-imputation.
  • Don't leak the formation-top columns at inference — they are training-only.
  • TVT ≠ TVD. The Z column is surface-relative elevation; TVT is the typewell's vertical coordinate. They differ by a per-region datum offset.
  • Direction is not monotone. A well's TVT can increase, decrease, or stay flat within a single lateral — any model that assumes monotonic TVT will fail on the steep tail.
  • DTW caveat: long sequences are O(N·M) in memory; for our 4000 × 2000 typical sizes use a Sakoe-Chiba band (constrained DTW) or down-sample by 4–8× before alignment.

10. Recommended Roadmap

  1. Reproduce report/baseline_rmse.csv → ship a constant-baseline submission.csv to confirm the format is correct.
  2. Build a DTW predictor over the assigned typewell → first credible submission.
  3. Stack 5–10 nearest typewells into a "super-typewell" → re-run DTW.
  4. Implement Tikhonov / Gauss–Newton inversion (see competition_overview.html §5). Initialize from DTW seed.
  5. Wrap in a sequential filter (particle or unscented Kalman) for online stability.
  6. Train a 1-D CNN / Transformer with formation-top heads as auxiliary supervision.
  7. Stretch goal: reproduce the Ambrus MDN approach — synthetic SVD pre-training + sequential candidate tracking. State-of-the-art per the 2022 NFES poster.
  8. Ensemble: classical inversion + ML; let ML learn the residual.
